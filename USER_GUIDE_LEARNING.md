# Decision Learning System - User Guide

## 🎯 What is the Decision Learning System?

The Decision Learning System helps you **learn from your past decisions** by tracking outcomes and reflecting on what worked and what didn't.

---

## 🚀 Quick Start

### Step 1: Make a Decision
1. Use the **Builder** tab to create a decision
2. Define criteria, add options, score them
3. Calculate results and save the decision
4. The decision is automatically added to your timeline with status: **Pending**

### Step 2: Implement the Decision
- Take action based on your decision
- Observe the real-world outcomes
- Wait for enough time to evaluate results (days, weeks, or months)

### Step 3: Review the Decision
1. Click the **Timeline** tab
2. Find your decision in the list
3. Click on it to open the detail view
4. Answer: "Was this decision correct?"
   - Click **👍 Yes** if it worked out well
   - Click **👎 No** if it didn't work as expected
5. Add reflection notes explaining why
6. Click **Save Review**

---

## 📊 Understanding the Timeline

### Timeline View
The Timeline shows all your decisions in chronological order with:
- **Date badge** - When the decision was made
- **Decision title** - What you were deciding
- **Metadata** - Number of criteria and options
- **Final choice** - What you selected
- **Status badge** - Current review status

### Status Badges

🟡 **Pending Review**
- Decision not yet reviewed
- Waiting for real-world outcome
- Yellow badge

🟢 **Correct Decision**
- Decision was successful
- Outcome met expectations
- Green badge

🔴 **Incorrect Decision**
- Decision didn't work out
- Learned from the mistake
- Red badge

## 💡 How to Write Good Reflection Notes

### For Correct Decisions ✅
Ask yourself:
- Why did this work?
- What criteria were most important?
- What would I do the same next time?
- What surprised me positively?

**Example:**
```
"React + Next.js was the right choice. The team ramped 
up quickly, and the ecosystem support exceeded 
expectations. The 'Ecosystem' criterion (20% weight) 
proved crucial. Would use similar weights for future 
tech stack decisions."
```

### For Incorrect Decisions ❌
Ask yourself:
- What went wrong?
- Which criteria did I underweight?
- What did I miss in the evaluation?
- What would I do differently?

**Example:**
```
"Expanding current office was wrong. We underestimated 
growth - ran out of space in 8 months. 'Space' should 
have been weighted 40%+ instead of 25%. Lesson: Don't 
optimize for short-term cost when growth is steep. 
Moving to Tech Hub would have been better."
```

## 📈 Using the Stats Dashboard

At the top of the Timeline, you'll see:

- **Total Decisions** - All decisions you've made
- **Reviewed** - Decisions you've evaluated
- **Correct** - Successful decisions (green)
- **Incorrect** - Unsuccessful decisions (red)
- **Pending** - Awaiting review (yellow)

### What to Look For:
- **High correct rate?** Your decision process is working!
- **Many pending?** Time to review some old decisions
- **Patterns in incorrect?** Look for common criteria issues

---

## 🎓 Learning from Your Decisions

### Pattern Recognition
After reviewing several decisions, look for:

1. **Criteria Patterns**
   - Which criteria matter most in successful decisions?
   - Are you consistently over/under-weighting certain factors?

2. **Option Patterns**
   - Do certain types of options tend to work better?
   - Are you being too conservative or too risky?

3. **Context Patterns**
   - What types of decisions do you excel at?
   - Where do you need to improve?

### Improving Future Decisions
Use your learnings to:
- Adjust criteria weights based on past outcomes
- Add new criteria you previously missed
- Be more thorough in option evaluation
- Trust your process when it works

## 🔄 Updating Reviews

Changed your mind about a review? No problem!

1. Open the decision from Timeline
2. Scroll to the review section
3. Click **Update Review**
4. Make your changes
5. Save again

---

## 💾 Data Privacy

- All decisions stored locally on your machine
- No external tracking or data sharing
- You control all your data
- Can delete decisions anytime from History tab

## 🎯 Best Practices

### 1. Review Regularly
- Set a monthly reminder to review pending decisions
- Don't wait too long - memories fade

### 2. Be Honest
- Mark decisions as incorrect when they fail
- Learning comes from acknowledging mistakes

### 3. Be Specific
- Write detailed reflection notes
- Include concrete examples and metrics

### 4. Look for Patterns
- Review your stats quarterly
- Identify trends in your decision-making

### 5. Iterate
- Use learnings to improve future decisions
- Adjust your criteria and weights based on outcomes

## 📝 Example Workflow

### Scenario: Choosing a Marketing Channel

**Week 1: Make Decision**
```
Decision: "Q1 Marketing Strategy"
Criteria: Reach (25%), Cost per Lead (30%), 
          Conversion (25%), Brand (20%)
Options: LinkedIn, Google Ads, Conferences
Choice: LinkedIn Ads + Content (Score: 87.0)
Status: 🟡 Pending
```

**Week 12: Campaign Ends**
- Gather results
- Analyze performance
- Compare to expectations

**Week 13: Review Decision**
```
Outcome: ✅ Correct
Notes: "LinkedIn delivered 150 qualified leads at 
$45/lead (target was $50). Conversion rate was 
12% (expected 10%). The high weight on 'Conversion 
Rate' was justified. Would use same approach for 
similar B2B campaigns."
Status: 🟢 Correct
```

**Week 14: Apply Learning**
- Use similar criteria weights for next campaign
- Trust the process
- Build on success

## 🆘 Troubleshooting

### "I don't see my decision in Timeline"
- Make sure you saved the decision
- Check the History tab to confirm it exists
- Refresh the Timeline tab

### "Can't click Save Review"
- You must select Yes or No first
- The button enables after selection

### "Want to change my review"
- Open the decision
- Click "Update Review"
- Make changes and save
